Mississippi doesn't write policy in a vacuum.

Phase 2 · Our Approach

We extended Phase 1 in three directions.

01

Multi-State Coverage

Expanded from Mississippi alone to seven states across the Southeast, focused on three regulated professions: medical, dental, and real estate.

02

Built to Stay Current

Crawlers for all 21 official sources are in place today. The weekly auto-refresh and change-alert loop is designed end-to-end and ready to activate when the system is deployed to production.

03

Specialized Research Agents

Beyond Q&A, the system now compares, benchmarks, and synthesizes. It does not just look things up.

How It Stays Current

From a regulator's website to your answer — in five steps.

Step 01

Weekly Check-In

Every Monday, the system wakes itself up and visits all 21 official regulator websites.

→
Step 02

Read & Compare

Reads each rule and compares it word-for-word with what we saw last week.

→
Step 03

Spot the Change

Identifies what's new, what was amended, and what was repealed since the last visit.

→
Step 04

Update the Library

Re-indexes anything that changed and removes rules that no longer exist.

→
Step 05

Notify the Team

Sends a weekly summary: "3 new rules, 1 amended, 0 repealed" — so a human can verify.

Built today

Steps 1 & 2 — the crawlers that read all 21 sources.

Ready to Activate

Steps 3, 4, 5 — the automation that turns reading into action. Architecture complete; activates on production deployment.

Scope · What's In, Where It Extends

Honest about what we have — and where the architecture extends.

A pilot has to draw a line somewhere. Here's where we drew ours, and where the same architecture takes you next.

In Scope Today

Verified
  • 7 states Mississippi, Tennessee, Alabama, Georgia, Arkansas, Texas, Louisiana
  • 3 agency types per state Medical boards, dental boards, real estate commissions
  • 21 official regulator pages monitored One per state-agency combination
  • Source: each state's SoS portal Administrative rules and the regulators that portal publicly links to

Where The Architecture Extends

Extensible
  • Direct board crawls Advisory opinions, disciplinary records, internal board policies
  • State statute corpora Unlocks the full authority-overreach detection capability
  • Additional agency types Nursing, insurance, contractors, cosmetology, and more
  • Additional states Beyond the Southeast pilot footprint

Each agency we add costs roughly $30 to load and $50/mo to maintain — same economics as adding a state.
